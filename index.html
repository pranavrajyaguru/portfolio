Autonomous Flight Systems

Autonomous Fixed-Wing UAV using ArduPilot

Designed and implemented a fixed-wing UAV by integrating the ArduPilot open-source autopilot ecosystem with a Pixhawk flight controller. The aircraft was developed for GPS-based waypoint missions while preserving manual override for takeoff, approach and landing.

Objective

Build a reliable autonomous aircraft capable of executing predefined waypoint missions with safe manual control available during critical flight phases.

Outcome

Demonstrated stable transition between manual and AUTO mode, GPS waypoint execution, telemetry communication and post-flight log analysis for performance evaluation.

Phase 01

Manual Flight Testing

Built and configured the fixed-wing airframe, then validated stability, propulsion, control surfaces and flight behavior through manual RC testing.

Phase 02

Autonomous Integration

Integrated Pixhawk, ArduPilot, GPS and telemetry; calibrated sensors, tuned parameters and developed waypoint missions in Mission Planner.
