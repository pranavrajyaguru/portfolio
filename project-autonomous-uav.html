Autonomous Flight Systems · Case Study 01

Autonomous Fixed-Wing UAV using ArduPilot

A GPS-guided fixed-wing aircraft built around Pixhawk and ArduPilot, with telemetry, waypoint missions and manual control for critical flight phases.

RoleDesign, integration & flight testing
PlatformFixed-wing RC aircraft
Flight stackPixhawk + ArduPilot
FocusAutonomous waypoint flight

The project

From a manually flown airframe to a repeatable autonomous mission.

Designed and implemented a fixed-wing UAV by integrating the ArduPilot open-source autopilot ecosystem with a Pixhawk flight controller. The goal was reliable GPS-based waypoint flight while retaining a safe manual override for takeoff, approach and landing.

01

Airframe validation

Verified propulsion, center of gravity, control-surface direction and stable manual flight before introducing autonomous control.

02

System integration

Installed Pixhawk, GPS and telemetry, calibrated sensors and configured flight modes and failsafe behavior.

03

Mission planning

Created GPS waypoint missions in Mission Planner and tuned the aircraft for dependable navigation and altitude control.

04

Flight analysis

Reviewed telemetry and flight logs to evaluate mission tracking, mode transitions and overall performance.

Outcome

A stable transition between pilot control and AUTO mode.

The project demonstrated waypoint execution, live telemetry communication, controlled flight-mode transitions and post-flight log analysis—forming a practical base for further autonomous aircraft experiments.
